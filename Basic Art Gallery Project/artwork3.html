About the Artwork

Annapurna, a significant work by Nandalal Bose, depicts the Hindu goddess of nourishment. The artwork likely captures the serene and benevolent aspect of the goddess, often shown holding a vessel of food and a spoon, symbolizing the provision of sustenance and well-being.

Bose's characteristic style, often incorporating fluid lines and a focus on essential forms, would be evident in this portrayal. The use of tempera would lend a unique texture and matte finish to the artwork, aligning with his artistic sensibilities.

About the Artist

Nandalal Bose was a pivotal figure in modern Indian art, known for his association with Rabindranath Tagore and his significant contributions to the Bengal School of Art. His artistic philosophy emphasized a return to indigenous artistic traditions and a deep connection with nature and Indian culture. Bose's works often showcase a blend of classical Indian aesthetics with modern interpretations, marked by their simplicity and expressive lines. His portrayal of Annapurna would reflect his unique artistic vision and his reverence for Indian mythology.
